Awareness is about:

  • acknowledging and being observant of cultural differences and similarities that exist across cultures
  • having self-awareness of own cultural values and beliefs, and how these impact therapeutic relating.

Sensitivity is about:

  • not assigning values or judgement to differences
  • keeping an enquiring attitude, an openness and flexibility in relation to others
  • having a respectful and professional approach to working with differences
  • not being ethnocentric, making assumptions or stereotyping.

Knowledge is about:

  • understanding core principles that underpin cultural difference
  • becoming familiar with some practices and beliefs, history, values and characteristics of other ethnic groups
  • having knowledge about how to engage, assess and treat issues with CALD clients in a service and clinical context.

Skills is about:

  • being able to apply the awareness, sensitivity and knowledge in practice, in an effective and appropriate way (including good communication, interviewing and relationship skills).
